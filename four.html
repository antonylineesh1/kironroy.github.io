Four Statements

Cash Flow Statement

Cash flow statement is the checkbook of the business. It covers a period of time vs. a snapshot (balance sheet)

Cash flow covers transactions from operations, financing, and investing Cash is a liquid asset on the Balance Sheet A = L + E

Liquidity is a termed used to denote whether or not a business is generating cash from its assets or convert its assets into cash quickly

Cash is not profit. Many business' can generate cash but don't make a profit, and profitable business that don't generate a whole lot of cash-flow.

When validating a business, most investors look at the cash-flow statement, rather than the income statement. To see if there is a healthy flow of money going into the business


Balance Statement

The Balance Statement is a snapshot (instance in time). It measures the financial condition of a business with respect to its total assets and claims or rights to those assets. Note the balance statement does not change over time.

Most important Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Owner Equity

Balance sheet insolvency : Assets < Liabilities + Owner Equity Think of a house that you bought for $200k but now is worth $140k. Even if you sell all your assets you cannot payoff the liabilities.

Balance sheet insolvency is different than cash flow insolvency, in that a business may have assets, but cannot convert the assets quickly enough to pay bills (Also known as liquidity).


Income Statement

Income statement shows booked income and the costs and expenses associated with making that income

Income statement DOES NOT show cash-flow. Cash flow is separate statement.

When validating a business, most investors look at the cash-flow statement, rather than the income statement. To see if there is a healthy flow of money going into the business


Earnings Statement

Retained Earnings Statement reports accumulated retained earning beginning of the year and the addition of any net income that will be added to that retained earnings.

Retained Earnings = Previous R.E. + Net Income - Dividends Paid

It also reports and dividends issued to investors. Dividends are cash payments made out to shareholders. Dividends are a type of cash out flow

Three things can be determined by the Earnings statement:

1. Give some or all money (dividends) to investors 2. Save the money for inside the business 3. Combination of 1 and 2

Retained earning does not report what is done with the retained earnings or cash-flow. It just reports the earnings

Business Operations

Financing, Operating and Investing are the three major activities in business operations Financing deals with how one gets started. When a business owner seeks a loan from a bank or SBA, issues stock to investors; the owner is part of the capital market.

Investing has to do with buying tools for a business. For example a cash register, machinery.

Operating activity takes care of day to day operations of a business by selling, creating products or providing services

Business creates a enormous amount of data. Think of how many sales a single Walmart makes in a single day!

Four fundamental financial documents represent this data: The income statement, cash-flow statement, the balance statement, and the earnings statement
